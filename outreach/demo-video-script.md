**Format:** Screen recording with text overlays (no voiceover needed, but optional)
**Resolution:** 1080p, browser fullscreen or near-fullscreen
**Music:** Upbeat, royalty-free (optional — the Pokemon cries add their own audio)

### SCENE 1 — Regular Mode Rapid Fire (0:03–0:12)
- **Action:** Start in Regular Mode with Dex + Gens order, shadows ON. Show the grid with shadow silhouettes visible. Type 3–4 well-known Pokémon names quickly (e.g., "Pikachu", "Charizard", "Mewtwo", "Eevee").
- **What to capture:**
  - Cards flipping from shadow silhouettes to revealed sprites
  - Pokemon cries playing on each reveal

## Recording Tips

1. **Pre-play before recording** — For the confetti shot, get a generation down to 1–2 remaining beforehand so you can trigger it quickly on camera.
2. **Use two browser windows** for the Duel/Co-Op shots — side by side at 50% width each, or use OBS picture-in-picture.
3. **Dark mode looks better on camera** — the colors pop more against the dark background.
4. **Keep typing fast** — the rapid-fire reveals are the most visually exciting part. Practice the names you'll type.
5. **Sound ON** — the Pokemon cries are a major selling point. Make sure they're audible in the recording.
6. **Don't pause too long** on any one shot — the video should feel snappy and energetic.
